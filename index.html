I'm Huy Hung LE, commonly known as "Hung".

My academic journey originally focused on Mathematics (Number Theory), but it was during my PhD studies in Mathematics in Caen, France, that I discovered my true passion for computer sciences and cybersecurity, leading to an pivotal decision to shift my career in that direction.

After successfully defending my PhD in November 2021, I worked as a versatile role employee at a restaurant Vietnamese in Caen, France. In addition to my part-time job, I dedicated myself to self-studying cybersecurity and enhancing related skills.

September 2022 marked a significant milestone as I enrolled in the Master program in Digital Security at Eurecom, Biot, France.

My primary interests within cybersecurity include Security analysis, Systems Administration, Malware analysis.

Beyond my academic pursuits, I love to play table tennis and chess. During my PhD studies in Caen, I actively participated in numerous chess competitions for teams and individuals. Currently, I am proudly to be a supervisor of a table tennis club in Eurecom.

Projects

  • Improve pestudio-cli tool
    Jun 2023 - Jul 2023
    https://github.com/HuyHung0/pestudio-cli

    This is a project of Forensics course at Eurecom.
    Pestudio-cli is a python-based command-line tool which can be used to check portable executable (PE) files for known malicious patterns. The tool stops working due to some changing in a library.
    The project is to fix the tool, improve it with new databases and add a new feature named "Hole detection" to detect whether there is any data "outside" of sections in a PE file.

  • Security Analysis of Bluetooth Low Energy Over-the-air Firmware Updates

    https://github.com/HuyHung0/Spring2023---Security-Analysis-Of-Bluetooth-Low-Energy-Over-The-Air-Firmware-Updates
    https://gitlab.eurecom.fr/lehh/spring2023-Security-analysis-of-Bluetooth-low-energy-Over-the-Air-firmware-updates

    This is a semester project at Eurecom.
    This project is to reverse engineering the protocol and analysis the securities of the firmware update processes over-the-air via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) of nrf51 and nrf52 chips (from Nordic Semiconductor).
